Reason

See which claims a piece of writing actually supports — and which it doesn't. Paste any dense text (a contract clause, an article, terms of service) and this extracts its claims, each linked to the exact sentence it's supposedly based on. Every linked quote is then checked against your original text automatically. If a claim's "evidence" isn't actually there, word for word, it's marked ungrounded — not hidden, not smoothed over.

What actually happens: Your text and your API key are sent directly from your browser to the AI provider you choose below — never through any server we operate, because we don't operate one. This page is a static file with no backend. We never see your key, your text, or the result. The grounding check (comparing each returned quote against your source text) happens entirely in your browser, after the response comes back, so a claim isn't taken on trust just because the model asserted it.

1. Choose a provider and supply your own key

2. Paste the text to examine

3. Claims, each checked against your text

Results will appear here after analysis.

Part of Collaborama. No account, no server, no stored key — the trust boundary is that nothing leaves your browser except the direct call to the provider you chose, with the key you supplied.
